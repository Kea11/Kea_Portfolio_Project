About Me

Keabetswe is a South African young woman who grew up in a small town in Soweto. She is a driven woman, is passionate about everything she does and is excited by new experiences and challenges. She also takes responsibility for the task completion.

I enjoy interacting with individuals and knowing their different experiences. I am very interested in people’s user experience, as it is very insightful and eye opening, and compliments the relevant technology world.

I am an ADA graduate

The African Developers Academy is a year long learnership, with a focus on teaching front-end development. I was one of the few women to make it through the selection process, being one of only 2.2%. Looking back, it has been an exciting experience, at times it was very overwhelming - and perhaps still is. Learning how to code has been the best thing I have ever done in my life and keeps me wanting to know more.

The ADA programme has been very intriguing, interesting, challenging and, at times, stressful but it has been all worth it because it taught me how to deal with and handle the pressure that comes with being a developer. All the projects we did, helped me improve my coding skills and prepare me for the outside world of front-end development. I recommend this programme to all African women of colour. Don’t miss out on this great experience of a lifetime!
